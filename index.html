    📘 What Does Activity Level Mean?

    Your activity level affects how many calories your body needs each day. Select the level that most accurately reflects your typical day:

    • Sedentary (1.2): You sit most of the day (e.g., desk job, no workouts).
    • Lightly Active (1.375): Light exercise or walking 1–3 days per week.
    • Moderately Active (1.55): Moderate workouts 3–5 days per week.
    • Very Active (1.725): Intense workouts nearly every day or a physically active job.
    • Extra Active (1.9): Very intense daily training or physical labor (e.g., athletes, construction).

    🎯 Understanding Fitness Goals

    Fitness goals guide how your calorie intake is divided into protein, fat, and carbs. Here’s what each plan prioritizes:

    • Balanced: A general health mix (30% Protein / 25% Fat / 45% Carbs)
    • Keto: Low-carb, high-fat for ketosis (25% Protein / 70% Fat / 5% Carbs)
    • Muscle Gain: Extra fuel for muscle growth (35% Protein / 25% Fat / 40% Carbs)
    • Weight Loss: Higher protein to protect muscle (40% Protein / 30% Fat / 30% Carbs)

    📝 How to Calculate Daily Calories for Weight Loss & Muscle Gain

    Introduction: Understanding your daily calorie needs is the first step toward any fitness goal—whether it's shedding fat, gaining muscle, or just staying healthy.

    🔍 What Are Calories?

    Calories are units of energy your body uses to function. From blinking to lifting weights, every action burns calories.

    📊 How to Calculate Daily Calories

    Your daily calorie needs are based on:

    • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) – how many calories you burn at rest
    • Activity Level – how active you are throughout the day

    Example: A 30-year-old male, 70 kg, 175 cm tall, moderately active needs about 2,650 calories/day.
